Assumptions baked into this ledger:
  • Rent grows at the annual rate you set; operating expenses are held flat for the whole hold — no expense inflation.
  • Mortgage interest on both the primary loan and any refinance amount is treated as fully deductible against this property's rental income at your marginal rate; this assumes you have other income for losses to offset against in early years.
  • The refinanced amount is modelled as interest-only debt secured against the other building — its balance never changes here, and it isn't repaid out of this property's sale proceeds.
  • Capital Cost Allowance (depreciation) is not factored into the tax calculation.
  • Selling costs (agent commission, legal fees, etc.) are not included in the resale numbers.
  • Capital gains tax at resale uses the inclusion rate above multiplied by your marginal rate — a simplification of Canadian rules, not tax advice.
  • Mortgage insurance premium rates follow CMHC's published schedule for owner-occupied 1–4 unit loans. Below 20% down, the tool assumes you'll live in one of the units (the only way the mortgage is insurable) and adds the CMHC premium to the loan automatically.
  • Cash-on-cash, total return %, ROI and IRR are all measured against actual cash invested: down payment plus any unfinanced premium and QST, minus the refinanced amount used to help fund it.
  • The IRR accounts for the timing of each year's cash flow plus the net proceeds at resale — a true annualized return, not just an average.
  • None of this is tax or lending advice — confirm current rules with a mortgage broker or accountant before relying on it for a real purchase.
